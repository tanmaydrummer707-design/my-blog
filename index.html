Clearing Core Doubts

Can NEET PG Be Cracked in Final Year?

Absolutely. Many toppers do it. Focus on high-yield topics and consistent revision.

Daily Study Hours

Aim for 6-8 hours daily, including breaks. Quality over quantity—study smart, not hard.

Balancing University Exams with NEET PG

Integrate both. Use university syllabus overlaps and allocate time wisely—NEET PG prep can reinforce your exams.

Is Coaching Necessary?

Not mandatory. Self-study with good resources works, but coaching provides structure if you need it.

What If I Start Late?

Start now. Even 3-4 months of focused prep can yield results. Prioritize essentials.

How Many Revisions?

At least 2-3 full revisions. Space them out for better retention.

Step-by-Step Preparation Roadmap

Phase 1: Concept Building (Months 1-2)

Master basics. Read standard textbooks, make notes, and understand fundamentals.

Phase 2: Revisions (Months 3-4)

Revise notes, focus on weak areas. Use mnemonics and diagrams.

Phase 3: PYQs and Test Analysis (Months 5-6)

Solve previous years' questions. Analyze mistakes and improve speed.

Phase 4: Mock Tests and Final Prep

Take full-length mocks weekly. Simulate exam conditions.
